Why image compression is essential for SEO

Large, uncompressed images are one of the most common reasons for slow websites. Search engines like Google track speed metrics such as Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) and Total Blocking Time (TBT). Heavy image files directly hurt these metrics, which can push your pages down in search results.

By using an online image compressor before uploading your pictures, you can:

  • Reduce page weight and loading time without sacrificing visible quality.
  • Improve Core Web Vitals and increase your chances of ranking higher.
  • Boost user satisfaction and decrease bounce rate.
  • Save bandwidth and hosting costs, especially on image-heavy sites.

If you run a Blogger, WordPress, or custom site, integrating compressed images into your workflow is one of the easiest and fastest SEO wins you can implement today.
